    Leslie Bega’s Age & Early Life

    Leslie Rae Bega was born on April 17, 1967, in Los Angeles, California, to Sephardi Jewish and Russian Jewish parents, Izahar Dick Bega and Marlene Ruth. Her youth was spent commuting between Los Angeles and New York. Bega attended the elite Lycée Français de Los Angeles in Los Angeles and graduated with honors. She studied film and theatre at the University of Southern California in Los Angeles, California. Leslie also attended the Marshall School of Business on Trousdale Pkwy in Los Angeles.

    Career

    Leslie made her Broadway debut at the age of six in the play The Patriots. Leslie portrayed the part of Maria Borges in the 1986 American comedy Head of the Class, opposite Hollywood superstar Brad Pitt. She appeared in 68 episodes of the series. Following that, she landed a role in the crime thriller C-16: FBI.

    Bega played Leah in three episodes of the immensely acclaimed criminal drama television series CSI: criminal Scene Investigation. In the internationally renowned criminal drama television series The Sopranos, she played Tony Soprano’s mistress, Valentina La Paz. She has appeared as a guest star in a number of television shows, including CSI: NY, Nip/Tuck, and American Dad! Her other significant appearances include Mobsters, a 1991 criminal drama, The American President, a 1995 comedic drama, and Lost Highway, a 1997 mystery-thriller. In 2001, Leslie had a solo performance at the Lincoln Center.
