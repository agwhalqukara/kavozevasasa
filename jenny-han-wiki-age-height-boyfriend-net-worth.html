    Jenny Han Professional Life

    Jenny Han authored her first book, the children’s novel Shug, during her college years, and it was released in 2006. Annemarie Wilcox, a twelve-year-old struggling to negotiate the pitfalls of junior high school, is the protagonist of the tale. During her summer breaks, Han later worked on a young adult romance trilogy about a girl’s coming-of-age.

    From 2009 to 2011, she worked on three novels released by Simon & Schuster: The Summer I Turned Pretty, It’s Not Summer Without You, and We’ll Always Have Summer. Furthermore, the trilogy was a New York Times Best Seller.

    She later released her second young adult trilogy, which she co-wrote with Siobhan Vivian. It appeared in the 2012 issue of Burn for Burn. The story revolves around three high school girls seeking vengeance in their island village, and it includes paranormal and romance aspects.

    In addition, in 2014, she published To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before, a young adult romantic novel. Lara Jean Song Covey is a high school student whose life is turned upside down when the letters she wrote to her five prior crushes are shipped without her knowledge.

    Furthermore, the book was picked up for a film adaptation within weeks of its release. The novel’s film adaptation, starring actress Lana Condor, began filming in July 2017 and was released by Netflix in August 2018, receiving excellent reviews.

    Han also makes a brief appearance in the film. P.S. I Still Love You and To All the Boys: Always and Forever were released in 2020 and 2021, respectively.

    A television series inspired on her novel series The Summer, I Turned Pretty was recently released on the OTP platform, Amazon Prime, in June 2022. Han will write, executive produce, and be a showrunner for the upcoming romantic dramedy streaming series XO, Kitty. The story will follow Lara’s sister, Kitty Covey, as she searches for true love.
